# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Collaboration Points
- **Product Manager**: Clarify acceptance criteria and technical feasibility. Provide estimates and identify technical constraints.
- **Project Manager**: Report progress, flag blockers, and participate in sprint planning.
- **UX Designer**: Collaborate on implementation to ensure design fidelity and discuss technical constraints.
- **DevOps Engineer**: Work on CI/CD integration, deployment automation, and troubleshooting production issues.
- **QA/Testing**: Partner on testability, automated testing frameworks, and defect resolution.

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Collaboration Points
- **Project Manager**: Align on timelines, resource constraints, and delivery commitments. Coordinate on risk management and stakeholder communication.
- **Developers**: Define acceptance criteria, clarify requirements, and validate technical feasibility.
- **UX Designer**: Partner on user research, validate design decisions, and define user journeys.
- **Business Analyst**: Collaborate on requirement gathering, prioritization, and business impact analysis.
- **Scrum Master**: Facilitate backlog refinement and ensure clarity on priorities and acceptance criteria.

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Collaboration Points
- **Product Manager**: Coordinate on delivery timelines, scope management, and stakeholder alignment.
- **Developers**: Track progress, remove blockers, and facilitate sprint planning.
- **Scrum Master**: Align on sprint execution, team capacity, and cross-team dependencies.
- **DevOps Engineer**: Coordinate release schedules and communicate deployment risks.
- **Business Analyst**: Ensure requirements clarity and support change management.

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters guide teams through Agile ceremonies, resolve impediments, and coach collaborative practices. They ensure smooth sprint execution, maintain team health, and foster continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate Agile ceremonies (sprint planning, daily standups, reviews, retrospectives)
- Remove impediments and blockers that prevent team progress
- Coach team members on Agile principles and self-organization
- Shield the team from external distractions and scope changes
- Track sprint metrics and team velocity
- Promote transparency and continuous improvement practices

### Goals
- Maximize team effectiveness and productivity
- Reduce cycle time and eliminate waste
- Foster a culture of collaboration and psychological safety
- Enable predictable sprint execution

### Typical Communication
- Daily facilitation of standups and tracking of blockers
- Sprint ceremonies and retrospective facilitation
- Regular alignment with Project Manager and Product Manager
- Team health and velocity reports

### Collaboration Points
- **Project Manager**: Coordinate on timelines, dependencies, and cross-team blockers. Share insights on team capacity and delivery predictability.
- **Product Manager**: Facilitate backlog refinement and sprint planning. Ensure clarity on acceptance criteria and priorities.
- **Developers**: Coach on Agile practices, remove impediments, and support self-organization.

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers own user research, wireframes, and usability testing. They ensure products are intuitive, accessible, and aligned with user needs through evidence-based design decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and synthesize insights
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Define user journeys and information architecture
- Perform usability testing and incorporate feedback
- Collaborate on acceptance criteria with a user-centric lens
- Maintain design systems and ensure consistency

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Validate design decisions through user feedback
- Reduce friction and improve user satisfaction
- Ensure design consistency across the product

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- User research presentations and journey maps
- Prototypes and design specs for implementation
- Usability test findings and recommendations

### Collaboration Points
- **Product Manager**: Partner to define user journeys, validate assumptions, and align design with product strategy. Contribute to acceptance criteria definition.
- **Developers**: Collaborate during implementation to ensure design fidelity and feasibility. Participate in design reviews.
- **QA/Testing**: Validate usability and design adherence during testing cycles.

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers manage CI/CD pipelines, automate deployment processes, and monitor system health. They bridge development and operations to enable fast, reliable, and secure releases.

### Responsibilities
- Build and maintain CI/CD pipelines and automation
- Manage infrastructure as code and cloud resources
- Implement monitoring, alerting, and observability
- Integrate security scanning and testing into workflows
- Coordinate deployments and rollback procedures
- Respond to incidents and performance issues

### Goals
- Enable fast, frequent, and safe deployments
- Minimize deployment failures and downtime
- Improve system reliability and observability
- Automate repetitive tasks and reduce manual effort

### Typical Communication
- Deployment plans and release coordination
- Incident response and post-mortem reports
- Infrastructure changes and capacity planning
- Pipeline improvements and automation updates

### Collaboration Points
- **Developers**: Integrate testing and security into workflows. Support local development environments and troubleshooting.
- **Project Manager**: Coordinate release schedules and communicate deployment risks. Provide deployment status and incident updates.
- **QA/Testing**: Ensure test automation is integrated into CI/CD. Collaborate on staging environments and smoke tests.

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts gather requirements, map stakeholder needs, and translate business problems into actionable backlog items. They bridge business and technical teams to ensure solutions meet organizational goals.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements
- Map stakeholder needs to technical solutions
- Create process flows, data models, and requirement specifications
- Facilitate requirements workshops and validation sessions
- Support backlog grooming and acceptance criteria definition
- Document processes and support continuous improvement

### Goals
- Ensure clear, complete, and testable requirements
- Bridge communication gaps between business and technical teams
- Reduce rework through early validation
- Support data-driven decision making

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documents and user stories
- Process maps and workflow diagrams
- Stakeholder interviews and validation sessions
- Backlog refinement and prioritization discussions

### Collaboration Points
- **Product Manager**: Partner to translate business needs into product features. Support prioritization with business impact analysis.
- **Project Manager**: Ensure requirements are clear and complete for planning. Support risk identification and change management.
- **Developers**: Clarify requirements and answer questions during implementation. Validate technical feasibility early.

---

## QA/Testing

### Role Summary
QA/Testing professionals validate quality through automated and manual testing, ensuring features meet acceptance criteria and quality standards before release.

### Responsibilities
- Design and execute test plans and test cases
- Perform functional, regression, and exploratory testing
- Develop and maintain automated test suites
- Report defects and verify fixes
- Validate acceptance criteria and Definition of Done
- Ensure test coverage and quality metrics

### Goals
- Ensure product quality and catch issues before production
- Reduce defect escape rate
- Improve test automation coverage
- Provide fast feedback to development teams

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and test case documentation
- Bug reports and quality metrics
- Test execution status and risk assessments
- Automation updates and coverage reports

### Collaboration Points
- **Developers**: Collaborate on testability and defect resolution. Support automated testing frameworks.
- **UX Designer**: Validate usability and design adherence during testing.
- **DevOps Engineer**: Integrate automated tests into CI/CD pipelines.
